About me

I’m Eldad Workeneh Tolla, a sophomore at Randolph College majoring in Computer Science and Mathematics with a minor in Data Science and Engineering. Originally from Ethiopia, my roots have profoundly shaped my perspective and passion for leveraging technology to drive meaningful change. Growing up, I was inspired by the potential of tech to empower communities and solve pressing challenges. My primary interests lie in AI, machine learning, and data-driven problem-solving. Through my coursework and hands-on projects, I’m building expertise in coding, algorithm design, and data analysis. I'm passionate about applying these skills to real-world challenges, especially those affecting underrepresented communities. As I continue my journey, I’m committed to creating a community that fosters innovation and inclusivity, helping others from similar backgrounds access opportunities in tech. With a vision of using technology for social good, I strive to be a bridge between my roots in Ethiopia and global advancements, ensuring that technology can be a tool for empowerment and progress.
